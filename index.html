The premise

Artificial intelligence is moving faster than our agreements about it. This lecture brings Australian AI research into a room with the people it affects, and leaves with better questions than it arrived with. We debate rather than conclude.

  1. Q1

    What role should AI play in creative work?

  2. Q2

    How do we balance innovation with ethical responsibility?

  3. Q3

    What choices do we want young people and communities to make as the technology develops?

Come if you are curious about creativity, emerging technology, education, ethics, or where all of this leaves us. No background in AI needed. Expect an hour of talk and argument, and plenty of room to disagree.

Who is speaking

Dr Rebecca Marrone

Senior Lecturer, Learning Sciences and Development
Centre for Change and Complexity in Learning, Adelaide University

A background in educational psychology, with research across creativity, wellbeing, and human and artificial cognition.

Dr Maria Vieira

Lecturer, School of Education
Centre for Change and Complexity in Learning, Adelaide University

A PhD in STEM and a Master's in the Psychology of Creativity. Her research covers creativity, human-AI collaboration, and gender equity in the STEM fields where women remain underrepresented.
